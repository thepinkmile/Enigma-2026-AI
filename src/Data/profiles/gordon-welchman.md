## Biography

William Gordon Welchman was born on 15 June 1906 in Fishponds, Bristol. He studied mathematics at Trinity College, Cambridge (BA 1929; MA 1933) and became a Fellow of Sidney Sussex College, teaching
geometry and algebraic number theory. He was recruited to GC&CS in late 1938 as one of the first mathematicians selected for cryptanalysis training before war broke out.

## Bletchley Park — Hut 6

Welchman was assigned to **Hut 6**, which handled Army and Air Force (Luftwaffe) Enigma traffic. He became head of Hut 6 in its early months and transformed it from a small experimental team into a
large-scale factory for Enigma cryptanalysis. Under his leadership, Hut 6 handled the **RED network** (Luftwaffe main network, broken regularly from 22 May 1940) and later Army networks.

His contributions extended beyond cryptanalysis to the **organisation of the entire Bletchley production line** — developing the systematic Hollerith card indexing of messages, the shift-work rotas,
and the traffic analysis methodology that made Hut 6 so productive.

### The Diagonal Board

Welchman's most technically significant contribution was the **Diagonal Board** (1940), an addition to Turing's Bombe design. Turing's original Bombe had a high false-stop rate — it would frequently
halt on incorrect settings, requiring manual checking. Welchman realised that the plugboard's symmetry (if A is connected to B, then B is connected to A) could be exploited to propagate additional
constraints through the Bombe's checking mechanism.

The Diagonal Board reduced false stops dramatically and made the Bombe practically viable at scale. Turing's biographer Andrew Hodges wrote that Turing told Welchman: *"I wouldn't have thought of
that."* The combined Turing-Welchman Bombe is the design that actually broke Enigma throughout the war.

## Post-War Career

After the war, Welchman moved to the United States and worked at:

- **MIT Lincoln Laboratory** — contributing to SAGE (Semi-Automatic Ground Environment), the first large-scale command-and-control system
- **MITRE Corporation** — senior analyst

In **1982**, he published *The Hut Six Story: Breaking the Enigma Codes* (McGraw-Hill), giving a detailed technical account of Hut 6's methods. The **NSA responded by withdrawing his US government
security clearances** and his MITRE consultancy was terminated. GCHQ also withdrew his UK clearances. The effect was described by his friends as *"devastating"* — isolating him from the community he
had worked in for decades.

Welchman wrote to the NSA protesting the decision; the correspondence was eventually declassified. He died of cancer on **8 October 1985**, aged 79, having seen the controversy over his book but not
its resolution.

A working replica of the Turing-Welchman Bombe was completed by John Harper and volunteers in **2008** and is now located at **TNMOC, Block H, Bletchley Park** — the most fitting possible memorial.
