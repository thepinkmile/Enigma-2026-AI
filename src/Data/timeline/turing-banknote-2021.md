## The Note

The **polymer £50 note** features:

- A photograph of **Alan Turing** from 1951 (taken by Elliott & Fry, National Portrait Gallery)
- Technical drawings from **Turing's 1936 paper** *On Computable Numbers* — the paper that defined the concept of the universal computing machine
- A table and formulae from Turing's **Hut 8 reports** — specifically the Banburismus work on Naval Enigma
- The **Pilot ACE computer** (Automatic Computing Engine), one of the first computers built in Britain, designed by Turing at the National Physical Laboratory
- A quote from Turing's 1949 *Times* newspaper interview: *"This is only a foretaste of what is to come, and only the shadow of what is going to be."*
- The ticker-tape notation from the Pilot ACE, spelling out Turing's birthday (23 June 1912) in binary

## The Launch Date

The note entered circulation on **23 June 2021** — the 109th anniversary of Alan Turing's birth on 23 June 1912. The Bank of England had announced Turing as the subject in July 2019, following a
public nomination process.

## Public Response

The announcement of Turing's selection in 2019 was broadly celebrated. It was read as a definitive national statement: the man who was prosecuted and destroyed by the British state was now to be
honoured on its currency — the highest-circulation public tribute available.

## The Rehabilitation Arc

The Turing banknote is the final point (to date) of a multi-decade arc of rehabilitation:

| Year | Event |
| ------ | ------- |
| 1954 | Turing dies; war work still classified |
| 1966 | ACM Turing Award established |
| 1974 | Ultra declassified; Turing's war role made public |
| 1983 | Hodges biography *Alan Turing: The Enigma* |
| 2009 | Gordon Brown government apology |
| 2013 | Royal Pardon (24 December) |
| 2014 | *The Imitation Game* film; global audience |
| 2017 | Alan Turing Law — pardons for all 49,000 |
| 2021 | £50 note enters circulation (23 June) |
