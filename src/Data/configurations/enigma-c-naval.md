## Overview

The Funkschlüssel C (FS-C) was the German Navy's first operational Enigma machine and the first Enigma deployed by any military service. Issued to the Reichsmarine in 1926, it represented a
significant departure from both the civilian Enigma A/B (which used 28-contact rotors and had two fixed rotors) and the later military standard. The FS-C had **28 contacts** (26 letters + 2 nulls) in
its rotors — unique among all Enigma variants — and a 5-rotor *Umschaltwalze* (switching reflector) at the left end.

**Historical significance:** The FS-C is the earliest military Enigma. When the German Navy introduced it in 1926, no other military power possessed an Enigma. It was replaced by the Enigma M series
  from 1934 onward.

## Breaking History

The Funkschlüssel C was studied and partially assessed by GC&CS when they purchased a commercial Enigma D in 1927. Hugh Foss assessed the commercial machine that year but naval traffic from the FS-C
is not known to have been broken during its service life. The navy transitioned to the more secure Enigma M series before WWII.

## Notes on Rarity

No known complete surviving Funkschlüssel C exists in public hands. The machine is primarily known from German Navy technical manuals and post-war intelligence assessment documents.
